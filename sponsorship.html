Inoru M. Wade (M.P.A) Founder and Executive Director of the Midwest Rainbow Research Institute


Friends, Now is a crucial time for LGBTQ+ Americans. Our visibility in the United States is at its highest level yet we face so many issues from bullying in our schools to discrimination to violent hate crimes and sexual assault. Although we have made progress in securing our equal rights in states across the nation, those victories have not extended across our region. Even in places where equal rights have been obtained under the law, lingering social woes and challenges remain.

I started my research as a graduate student in the number one ranked school in the nation for public policy at the local level. Being the only student in the history of the school to study issues and affairs concerning the LGBTQ+ community, I quickly learned how vital professional academic research was to progressing the community forward. Upon graduating I continued to have a deep connection to the Midwest and started to volunteer myself to give presentations and speeches regarding the work I did as a graduate student. It became very clear to me seeing the issues we face as a community that more needed to be done.

This is why I started the Midwest Rainbow Research Institute, a grassroots organization operating exclusively for the LGBTQ+ community. We will dedicate ourselves to continuing LGBTQ+ research, producing data, and informing local leaders across the region of the issues that we face as a community. We have already had tremendous success from leading training programs for Emergency Medical Service employees in Kansas to speaking at national conferences such as PFLAG and The Reformation Project and being called upon to testify in city halls around nondiscrimination ordinances. We want to go even further than speaking. By providing research and data in the form of surveys and publications, we will be able to arm our local leaders, organizations, and communities with the information they need to serve and advocate for our community better.

This will not be easy, and that is why we need your assistance. Through our sponsorship program you will be funding our research, surveys, publications, and speaking engagements so that we can impact as many LGBTQ+ lives as possible. We encourage you to look into our sponsorship guide and aid us as we promote equality through enlightenment.

Sincerely,



Inoru Wade
